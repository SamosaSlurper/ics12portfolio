Recursion

Assignment Intro

Recursion looked pretty difficult at first glance, but after we understood the core concept it wasn't really that bad. For this unit, we just used methods to call themselves to solve problems by breaking them down into smaller sub-problems. I chose the assignment where we wrote an iterative method to sum numbers inclusively, and then recursively as it was probably the best of the unit.

Programming Concept

The concept of recursion is pretty straightforward once you understand the base case. Basically, a recursive method calls itself with different paramaters until it fulfills a specific condition that stops the loop from running. In the photo, we see how an iterative loop uses a standard for-loop to keep adding to a total. The recursive method does the exact same thing but calls itself with n - 1 until it reaches 1.

Challenges

The biggest challenge was definitely wrapping my head around the concept of base case. Without a proper base case, the method would just run infinitely and crash the program, which happened many times to say the least.

Solutions

I sat down and walked myself through the loop step by step using a sheet of paper to track the loops progress to find the error and avoid the infinite loops.

Reflection

Overall, recursion was pretty challenging but I was able to undertand it for the most part eventually. However, it does help make complicated algorithms a lot simpler and cleaner to write in comparison to traditional loops.
